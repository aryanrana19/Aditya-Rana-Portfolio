Major Project

Design, Development, Fabrication, and Testing of Cold Weather Air-Based Body Heating System

The Cold Weather Air-Based Heating System (CWABHS) significantly advances the Extended Cold Weather Clothing System (ECWCS) by reducing clothing layers required for optimal thermal comfort. It integrates flexible piping and a portable air heater above existing layers to distribute heated air uniformly. Smart, personalized controls allow users to adjust temperatures based on preferences and conditions, ensuring adaptability and efficiency. Testing confirms CWABHS provides reliable thermal comfort, enhances mobility, and maintains energy efficiency, making it a practical solution for cold environments.

Exploratory Project

Designing an electric trike as a mechanical engineer involves combining innovation, functionality, and sustainability to meet modern transportation demands. With urbanization driving congestion and pollution, electric trikes provide an eco-friendly alternative, offering zero emissions, reduced noise, and smooth acceleration. Key design priorities include optimizing the frame, suspension, and braking systems for performance, safety, and ergonomic comfort. Features like intuitive controls, stability systems, and enhanced visibility ensure a secure, user-friendly experience. Using lightweight materials, efficient batteries, and regenerative braking minimizes environmental impact. Collaboration across disciplines integrates mechanical, electrical, and software components seamlessly. Electric trikes are especially beneficial for seniors, individuals with disabilities, and novice riders, delivering stability and accessibility for diverse urban populations.

Minor Project

Design and development of a Multipurpose Cooking Chulha (Stove)

This project focuses on the meticulous design and development of a multipurpose cooking Chulha (stove) that optimizes energy use, enhances durability, and ensures user-centric functionality for both rural and urban households. Incorporating advanced thermal engineering and ergonomic design, the Chulha (stove) maximizes fuel efficiency, minimizes emissions and ensures robust performance under varied conditions. Its innovative features reduce fuel consumption, promote cost savings, and support environmental sustainability by lowering carbon emissions.
